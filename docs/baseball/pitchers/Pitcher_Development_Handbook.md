# Pitcher Development Handbook

## Pitch Better, Think Better: Using PSIS to Develop Consistent Pitching

*A practical handbook for competitive pitchers. No app required.*

---

## Introduction

You stand on the mound alone. The crowd, the scoreboard, and your ERA only tell part of the story.

This handbook teaches you how to think about your outings the way **EABR** (End of At-Bat Result) was designed — one hitter at a time, with honest weight for damage and for stranded runners.

**PSIS** is software that records this thinking. **This book is the thinking itself.**

The goal is not better statistics. **The goal is becoming a better pitcher.**

---

## Part 1 — Why This Way of Thinking Exists

Traditional stats — ERA, WHIP, wins — summarize your season. They do not remember:

- The three strikeouts before the bad inning
- The ground outs that escaped a jam
- The walks that hurt more than the one hit

PSIS evaluates **each plate appearance** because that is how baseball is actually played: one confrontation at a time.

**Key idea:** You are not your last inning. You are the sum of every at-bat you learn from.

## Part 2 — One At-Bat at a Time

### The rule

```
One hitter. One plan. One execution. One result. Reset.
```

### Why it matters

- Focus shrinks to what you control
- Bad at-bats do not have to become bad innings
- Good at-bats stack when you stay present

**Mental reset at the rubber:** breathe, state your plan, compete on pitch one.

*Expanded: [Thinking_One_At_Bat_At_A_Time.md](./Thinking_One_At_Bat_At_A_Time.md)*

## Part 3 — EABR From the Mound

### You win (good unit)

- Strikeout
- Fly out
- Ground out (including double and triple plays — still 1 good unit per at-bat)

### They win (bad unit)

- Walk
- Hit (single, double, triple, home run)

### Extra weight

| Situation | Extra units |
|-----------|-------------|
| Runs score on their at-bat | +1 bad unit per run (RBI) |
| Third out with runners on | +1 good unit per stranded runner (LOB) |

### Your net score

**Delta** = good units − bad units (inning or outing)

Positive delta = you won more fights than you lost.

*Expanded: [Understanding_EABR_As_A_Pitcher.md](./Understanding_EABR_As_A_Pitcher.md)*

## Part 4 — Process Beats One Result

| You control | You do not always control |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Plan, execution, compete | Where batted balls land |
| Reset after bad pitches | Defense on that play |
| Long-term habits | One night's box score |

Great execution sometimes gets a bad unit (hard single). Poor execution sometimes gets a good unit (weak pop-up). **Do not confuse one outcome with your process.**

Long-term winners improve **how many at-bats they win** — week after week.

## Part 5 — Learning From Every Outing

### Next-day review (5 steps)

1. **Delta** — win more at-bats than you lost?
2. **Innings** — where did it turn?
3. **Patterns** — what repeats?
4. **Adjustments** — one fix only
5. **Questions** — ask your coach with specifics

### Questions to always ask

- What was my delta, and does it match how I felt?
- Which at-bat hurt most, and why?
- Where did I strand runners?
- What do I keep, and what do I change?

*Expanded: [Learning_From_Every_Outing.md](./Learning_From_Every_Outing.md)*

## Part 6 — Consistency Over One Great Day

One dominant outing does not make a career. **Trending positive delta over a month** does.

| Measure | Timeframe |
|---------|-----------|
| One outing | One lesson |
| 2–4 outings | Pattern forming |
| A month | Real development signal |
| A season | Your arc |

Routine, count management, and emotional reset between at-bats build consistency.

*Expanded: [Building_Consistency.md](./Building_Consistency.md)*

## Part 7 — Confidence That Survives Bad Games

Bad games happen. **Data anchors you** when emotion lies.

| You feel | Data might show |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Terrible" | Positive delta except one inning |
| "Fine" | Negative delta — fix walks or damage |
| "Always collapse" | LOB credit — you fight in jams |

Steps after a bad outing: feel it briefly → separate outing from identity → name one win → name one fix → show up tomorrow.

Compare **you to you** — not to teammates without context.

*Expanded: [Confidence_Through_Data.md](./Confidence_Through_Data.md)*

## Part 8 — Self-Development Habits

| Habit | When |
|-------|------|
| At-bat reset | Every hitter |
| Two-sentence journal | After every game |
| One bullpen focus | Every pen |
| Trend talk with coach | Every 2–4 weeks |

### Good goals

- Positive delta in 3 of 4 outings
- Fewer walks over a month
- First-pitch strikes

### Bad goals

- Perfect ERA
- Never give up a homer
- More strikeouts than teammate X

## Part 9 — Misconceptions (Quick Answers)

| Question | Short answer |
|----------|--------------|
| 8 Ks but bad EABR? | Walks, hits, and RBI bad units count too |
| ERA down, EABR flat? | Different measures — both can be true |
| One bad inning? | Inning deltas isolate it — you are not defined by it |
| Why LOB? | Stranding runners is a pitching win |
| Strikeouts only? | Fly outs, ground outs, LOB all matter |

*Full FAQ: [Common_Pitcher_Misconceptions.md](./Common_Pitcher_Misconceptions.md)*

---

## Part 10 — Your Commitment

PSIS does not ask for perfection. It asks for:

1. **Honesty** — own walks and mistakes
2. **Presence** — one at-bat at a time
3. **Patience** — trends over weeks
4. **Partnership** — work with your coach and the data

> **Pitch better by thinking better. Think better by reviewing every outing without fear.**

You do not need the app to live this way. If your staff uses PSIS, you now speak the same language they do.

**Keep competing. Keep learning. The next at-bat is yours.**
