# Pitching Is a Process

Results flash on the scoreboard. **Process** is what you repeat when nobody is watching.

---

## Results vs Process

| Results | Process |
|---------|---------|
| Runs on the board | Your plan, execution, and compete level |
| ERA for the night | How many at-bats you won |
| Win or loss | Whether you improved something fixable |

**You cannot always control results. You can always control process.**

PSIS separates them by logging **every at-bat** — not just the final run total.

---

## Execution vs Outcome

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Execution** | Did you throw the pitch you intended, with intent? |
| **Outcome** | What the hitter did with it |

They do not always match.

---

## When Great Execution Produces a Bad Result

| Situation | What EABR sees | What you should think |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Perfect slider, hard single | Bad unit | "Good pitch. Sometimes hits happen." |
| Good sinker, weak grounder, booted | Bad unit if hitter reaches | "Defense affects outcome — still compete." |
| Best fastball of the night, homer | Bad units + RBI | "Right idea, wrong location or count — adjust." |

**Do not trash your process because of one outcome.**  
**Do not ignore outcomes forever.** Patterns over time tell you if the process works.

---

## When Poor Execution Produces a Good Result

| Situation | What EABR sees | What you should think |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Hanging curve, weak pop-up | Good unit | "I got lucky — fix the pitch." |
| Constantly behind in count, jam job for out | Good unit | "Outcome saved me — count discipline matters." |
| Walks the bases loaded, double play | Good unit + stranded LOB possible | "Defense saved me — I need strikes." |

A good unit on a bad pitch is **not permission** to repeat the mistake.

---

## Why Long-Term Process Wins

One outing is noise. A season of at-bats is signal.

| Short-term trap | Long-term truth |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "I gave up 5 runs — I'm terrible" | Check delta: maybe you won more at-bats than you lost |
| "I threw a shutout — I'm fixed" | Check process: were you efficient or lucky? |
| "Strikeouts are my only value" | Ground outs and stranded runners stack good units too |

Development is **trending better at winning at-bats** — with your coach, over weeks.

---

## The Process Checklist (After Any Outing)

Ask yourself honestly:

1. Did I have a plan for each hitter?
2. Did I execute in the strike zone when ahead?
3. Did I compete with runners on?
4. Did I reset after bad at-bats?
5. Did my sequences make sense?

EABR answers **how many at-bats you won**. This checklist answers **how you pitched**.

---

## What Your Coach Sees

Coaches love process because it is **teachable**:

- Location
- Sequences
- Count management
- Body language between innings

PSIS gives them numbers. Film gives them proof. **You** bring the willingness to connect both.

---

## The Pitcher's Mantra

> "I control the next pitch. I learn from the last at-bat. I am not my last result."
