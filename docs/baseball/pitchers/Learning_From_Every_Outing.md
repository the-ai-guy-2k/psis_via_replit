# Learning From Every Outing

Every game is a lesson — if you know how to read it.

---

## Review Within 24 Hours

Best time: **the day after**, with your coach or on your own with notes.

| Too early (locker room) | Just right (next day) |
|-------------------------|----------------------|
| Emotion high | Emotion lower |
| Blame easy | Patterns visible |
| Memory distorted | Coach has EABR summary ready |

---

## The Five-Part Review

### 1. Delta — Did I win more at-bats than I lost?

| Delta | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Positive | More good units than bad — pitcher-favorable outing at the at-bat level |
| Negative | More bad units — hitter won more fights |
| Near zero | Battle even — small improvements matter |

**This is not your ERA.** It is your **battle record** for the outing.

---

### 2. Innings — Where did the game turn?

Look inning by inning:

| Question | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Which inning had the worst delta? | Find the cluster, not the whole game |
| Which inning had the best delta? | Repeat what worked |
| Did I strand runners or let innings snowball? | LOB good units = you fought back |

**One bad inning should not define you** — but you must **name** it to fix it.

---

### 3. Patterns — What repeats?

| Pattern type | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| **Success** | First-pitch strikes leading to good units |
| **Mistake** | Walks after falling behind 2-0 |
| **Sequence** | Fastball-slider combo winning; changeup up getting hit |
| **Situation** | Runners on — nibbling vs attacking |

Write one sentence: *"When I ___, good things happen."*  
Write one sentence: *"When I ___, bad units stack."*

---

### 4. Adjustments — One or two fixes only

Do not rebuild your delivery after one start.

| Good adjustment | Bad adjustment |
|-----------------|----------------|
| "First-pitch cutter to their hands" | "Throw everything harder" |
| "Two-strike slider below zone" | "Never throw changeups again" |

Pick **one mechanical** and **one mental** focus for the next bullpen.

---

### 5. Questions to Ask After Every Outing

Ask your coach — or yourself:

1. What was my delta, and does it match how I felt?
2. Which at-bat hurt most, and what pitch caused it?
3. Where did I strand runners — what can I repeat?
4. Did my sequences match the game plan?
5. What is **one** thing I keep, and **one** thing I change?

---

## Successes You Might Overlook

| You might dismiss… | EABR might show… |
|--------------------|------------------|
| "I got shelled" | Positive delta except one inning |
| "I only went 4 innings" | High good-unit count per at-bat |
| "I didn't strike anyone out" | Ground outs and LOB credit stacking wins |

Celebrate real wins. Fix real problems. Do not let the scoreboard alone write your story.

---

## Mistakes Worth Owning

| Own it | Let it go |
|--------|-----------|
| Walks from falling behind | Weak single on a good pitch |
| Hanging breaking balls | Defender error (talk separately) |
| Losing focus after a homer | One bloop on a decent sequence |

Ownership builds trust with your coach. **Excuses block development.**

## Without PSIS — Same Method

| Inning | Result | Good? | Bad? | Note |
|--------|--------|-------|------|------|

The **habit** matters more than the app.
