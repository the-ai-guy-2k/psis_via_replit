# Why PSIS Exists for Pitchers

You stand on the mound alone. Everyone sees the scoreboard. Few people see what you actually did — at-bat by at-bat.

PSIS exists to close that gap **for your development**, not to add pressure.

---

## The Scoreboard Lie

After a game, people look at:

- **ERA** — how many earned runs you allowed
- **Wins and losses** — whether your team won
- **Hits and walks** — how much traffic you allowed

Those numbers matter. They are also **incomplete**.

| What the box score says | What it hides |
|------------------------|---------------|
| "He gave up 4 runs" | You struck out six and stranded five runners in another inning |
| "Clean line" | You were behind in the count all night and got lucky on weak contact |
| "Short outing" | One bad inning erased three good ones |

Traditional stats summarize the **result**. They rarely tell the story of **each fight with each hitter**.

---

## Why You Should Care About Individual At-Bats

Baseball is not one game. It is a chain of plate appearances:

```
Hitter 1 → Hitter 2 → Hitter 3 → … → Hitter 27
```

You win some. You lose some. Development happens when you know **which ones** and **why**.

| Reason | For you |
|--------|---------|
| **Memory lies** | You remember the home run. You forget the three quality outs before it. |
| **Confidence needs evidence** | "I threw well" is stronger when you can point to wins, not just feelings. |
| **Fixes need targets** | "Get better" is vague. "Stop falling behind 2-0" comes from patterns. |
| **One inning is not you** | A bad third inning does not erase a strong first and fourth. |

PSIS evaluates pitching **one at-bat at a time** so your improvement has a foundation — not just a feeling.

---

## Why Improvement Starts With Each Confrontation

Every hitter is a new problem:

- One plan
- One execution
- One result

When you think in at-bats, you control what you can control:

| You control | You do not control |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Your plan | Where the ball lands after contact |
| Your effort | Umpire zones |
| Your response after a bad pitch | Sometimes bloops and bleeders |
| Competing with runners on | Your defense on that play |

EABR (End of At-Bat Result) records whether **you won that confrontation** — with extra weight when damage happens or when you strand runners.

That is not punishment. It is **feedback**.

---

## What PSIS Is Not Trying to Do

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "PSIS replaces ERA" | No — ERA still matters to the team |
| "PSIS is only for strikeout pitchers" | Ground outs, fly outs, and stranded runners all count |
| "One bad EABR outing means I'm failing" | Trends over weeks matter, not one night |
| "PSIS judges me as a person" | It logs at-bats. You are more than a line. |

---

## Why Your Coach Cares

Your coach uses PSIS (or the same ideas on paper) to:

- Give you specific feedback instead of vague praise
- Show you when your process is improving before ERA catches up
- Build a shared language: good units, bad units, delta, stranded runners

You do not have to love the system on day one. You do have to understand **why** it exists: **so you can see yourself clearly and get better faster.**

---

## The Pitcher's Version

> "I am not my last inning. I am the sum of every at-bat I learn from."

That is why PSIS exists for you.
