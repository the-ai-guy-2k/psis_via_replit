# Thinking One At-Bat at a Time

The mound is loud. The game is long. The only thing you can pitch is **this hitter, right now**.

---

## The Rule

```
One hitter.
One plan.
One execution.
One result.
Then forget it and move on.
```

Not "I need a shutout." Not "I can't give up another run." Not "what if they pull me."

**This at-bat.**

---

## Why It Works

### Baseball rewards presence

The pitcher who lives in the third inning while pitching the sixth gives away focus. The pitcher who wins the current at-bat stacks wins.

### Pressure shrinks to one battle

| Big picture thought | At-bat thought |
|--------------------|----------------|
| "Don't blow the lead" | "Fastball in, slider away" |
| "My ERA is climbing" | "Compete this pitch" |
| "Coach is watching" | "One hitter" |

EABR scores each at-bat separately. Your job matches the scoring: **win the one in front of you**.

---

## What "One Plan" Means

Before the first pitch, know:

1. **How you are attacking this hitter** (inside, away, soft, hard)
2. **Your put-away pitch** if you get two strikes
3. **What you will do if you fall behind**

You are not guessing. You are executing a plan — pitch by pitch, at-bat by at-bat.

---

## What "One Execution" Means

After you choose the pitch, commit:

- Full intent
- Full conviction
- No half-speed because you are worried about the last at-bat

A hung slider is one bad unit. Dwelling on it during the next hitter's at-bat can become two.

---

## What "One Result" Means

When the at-bat ends, classify it honestly:

| Result | What happened |
|--------|---------------|
| **You won** | Strikeout, fly out, ground out — good unit |
| **They won** | Walk, hit — bad unit (more if runs scored) |
| **You escaped** | Inning ends with runners on — extra credit for stranding them |

Then **reset**. The result is data. It is not a verdict on your career.

---

## Mental Discipline Through the Outing

| Inning situation | At-bat focus |
|------------------|--------------|
| Quick 1-2-3 | Same intensity — do not cruise |
| Bases loaded | One pitch at a time — LOB credit exists if you escape |
| Big lead | Attack — bad habits form when you coast |
| Close game | Trust your plan — not the scoreboard |

PSIS logs innings separately on purpose. Your mind should too: **finish this inning's at-bats, then breathe.**

---

## When the Last At-Bat Was Bad

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Note what pitch or count hurt you | Carry shame to the next hitter |
| Trust your coach's between-inning talk | Replay the whole game in your head |
| Compete on pitch one of the next at-bat | Change your whole identity because of one hit |

One bad at-bat is one bad unit. The next at-bat is a new chance for a good unit.

---

## When the Last At-Bat Was Good

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Repeat your process | Assume the next hitter will be easy |
| Stay aggressive in the zone | Get cute because you are "dealing" |

Good units stack. They do not guarantee the next one. **Stay in the at-bat.**

---

## Practice: The At-Bat Reset

Between hitters, try a physical reset:

1. Breathe once at the rubber
2. Say your plan in one sentence
3. Stare at the catcher's sign — nothing else

This is how PSIS thinks. This is how great pitchers think.
