# Building Consistency

One dominant outing does not make a career. **Repeatable quality** does.

---

## What Consistency Means for a Pitcher

| Consistency is… | Consistency is not… |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Winning more at-bats than you lose over time | One no-hitter |
| Stable EABR delta across outings | Perfect ERA every start |
| Same plan, same compete, same reset | Identical stat line every game |
| Improving your worst innings | Never having a bad inning |

---

## Why One Great Outing Can Lie

| Great line | Hidden truth |
|------------|--------------|
| Low ERA start | Negative delta — hitters won more fights |
| Many strikeouts | Walks and hard contact in other at-bats |
| "Felt amazing" | Sequences that will not work twice |

Enjoy great days. **Do not build your identity on one of them.**

---

## Why PSIS Rewards Repeatable Quality

PSIS tracks **sessions over time**:

- Session delta
- EABR fraction (good units ÷ bad units)
- Inning-by-inning patterns

| Trend | Message |
|-------|---------|
| Delta rising over 4 outings | Process is working — stay the course |
| Fraction stable above 1.0 | You win more fights than you lose — consistently |
| Wild swings (+5, -4, +2, -3) | Fix focus or execution between at-bats |

Your coach sees the trend. **You should too.**

---

## How to Measure Improvement (Weeks and Months)

| Timeframe | What to watch |
|-----------|---------------|
| **One outing** | One lesson — not a verdict |
| **2–4 outings** | Early pattern — walk rate, first-pitch strikes |
| **Full month** | Delta trend, consistency inning to inning |
| **Season** | Development arc — am I winning more at-bats than last year? |

ERA will move slower than EABR. **Trust the at-bat trend first.**

---

## Building Blocks of Consistency

### 1. Routine

Same pre-pitch breath. Same between-inning focus. Same post-outing review habit.

### 2. Plan discipline

Do not reinvent yourself each hitter. Execute the staff game plan.

### 3. Count management

The most consistent pitchers **get ahead** and **finish**.

### 4. Emotional reset

Bad at-bat → next hitter. Bad inning → next inning. **No carryover.**

### 5. Physical preparation

Sleep, hydration, arm care — consistency off the field supports consistency on it.

---

## The Consistency Scoreboard (In Your Head)

After each outing, rate 1–5:

| Area | Question |
|------|----------|
| Plan | Did I know what I was doing each at-bat? |
| Execute | Did I throw with intent? |
| Compete | Did I battle with runners on? |
| Reset | Did I bounce back after damage? |

EABR delta is the **objective** version. These four are the **daily** version.

---

## When Consistency Feels Boring

Boring is good. **Boring means your team can count on you.**

The pitcher who is brilliant one week and lost the next does not get the ball in big spots. The pitcher with **steady positive delta** does.
