# PSIS Pitcher Development Philosophy

**Become a better pitcher — one at-bat at a time.**

---

## What This Is

This folder is a **pitcher development handbook**, not software documentation. It explains how to think about your outings the way PSIS and EABR were designed to support — from the mound, not from a keyboard.

You do not need to install anything to learn from these pages. Your coach may use PSIS to log games; this documentation teaches you **what those logs mean** and **how to use that thinking to improve**.

---

## What This Is Not

| Not this | Because |
|----------|---------|
| App tutorial | That is for operators and coaches |
| Coach-only theory | See `docs/baseball/coaches/` for staff-level philosophy |
| A grade on your worth | EABR measures at-bats, not who you are |

**The goal is not better statistics. The goal is becoming a better pitcher.**

## Quick Vocabulary

| Term | What it means for you |
|------|----------------------|
| **At-bat** | One confrontation with one hitter until it ends |
| **EABR** | End of At-Bat Result — who won that confrontation |
| **Good unit** | You won the at-bat |
| **Bad unit** | The hitter won the at-bat |
| **Delta** | Your net score (good minus bad) for an inning or outing |
| **LOB** | Runners you stranded when the inning ended — that is a win |

---

## A Note From the Philosophy

PSIS does not ask you to be perfect. It asks you to be **honest, consistent, and forward-looking**. One bad inning does not define you. A full season of at-bats tells the truth about your development — if you learn from each one.
