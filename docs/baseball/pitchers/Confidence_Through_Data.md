# Confidence Through Data

Bad games happen. **How you respond** defines your development.

---

## The Emotional Trap

After a rough outing:

| Feeling | Danger |
|---------|--------|
| "I'm not good enough" | You overcorrect or shut down |
| "Stats hate me" | You ignore real fixes |
| "Coach is done with me" | You pitch scared next time |
| "I need to be perfect" | You nibble and walk people |

Emotion is human. **Data is your anchor.**

---

## What Data Can Do for Confidence

EABR and session summaries can show:

| You feel… | Data might show… |
|-----------|------------------|
| "I was terrible" | Positive delta except one inning |
| "I got lucky" | Negative delta despite low ERA |
| "I always collapse" | LOB credit in half your innings — you fight |
| "Strikeouts are all I have" | Ground outs and fly outs winning too |

Data does not replace your coach's voice. It **backs up** the truth when your brain lies.

---

## Handling a Bad Game

### Step 1: Feel it briefly

You care. Good. Do not pretend you do not.

### Step 2: Separate the outing from your identity

| You are not… | You are… |
|--------------|----------|
| One ERA | A developing pitcher |
| One homer | Someone who can reset |
| Tonight's line | Your next outing's competitor |

### Step 3: Find one good unit story

Even bad nights have strikeouts, ground outs, or stranded runners. **Name one win.**

### Step 4: Name one fix

Not ten. **One** pitch, count, or mental habit.

### Step 5: Show up tomorrow

Confidence is built in bullpens and film — not in spiraling.

---

## Avoiding Emotional Reactions to One Number

| Number | Healthy response |
|--------|------------------|
| Negative session delta | "Which innings? Which counts? What's the fix?" |
| Grand slam bad units | "That at-bat hurt. Next at-bat is new." |
| Great positive delta, high ERA | "Process worked — balls will fall less over time." |
| Flat delta | "I battled even — find the tiebreaker to improve." |

**Never** let one inning erase a month of trend.

---

## Building Confidence That Lasts

| Fragile confidence | Durable confidence |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| "I threw a shutout" | "I win at-bats when I execute my plan" |
| "They didn't score" | "I stranded six runners in two innings" |
| "I struck out 10" | "My delta is positive in 4 of my last 5 outings" |

PSIS helps you build **durable** confidence — tied to process you control.

---

## When Data Hurts (And What to Do)

Sometimes the numbers confirm the bad feeling. That is **useful pain**.

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Negative delta matches how you felt | Own it with coach — build a plan |
| Walks stacking bad units | Count work in bullpen |
| RBI bad units clustering | Location with runners on — game sim |

Data should **clarify**, not **shame**. If it feels like shame, talk to your coach — that is a culture problem, not a math problem.

---

## Separating One Performance From Development

```
One outing  =  a chapter
A month     =  a theme
A season    =  your story
```

You are allowed one bad chapter. You are not allowed to **stop reading**.

---

## A Note on Comparison

Do not compare your delta to another pitcher's without context:

- Different opponents
- Different roles (starter vs reliever)
- Different sample sizes

Compare **you** to **you** — last month vs this month.
