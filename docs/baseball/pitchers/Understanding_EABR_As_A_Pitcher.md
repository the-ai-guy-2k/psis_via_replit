# Understanding EABR as a Pitcher

EABR = **E**nd of **A**t-**B**at **R**esult.

When the at-bat ends, the system asks one question: **Did you win, or did the hitter win?**

This page explains the rules from **your** side of the mound.

---

## The Simple Picture

| You get… | EABR calls it… |
|----------|----------------|
| Strikeout | **Good** — 1 good unit |
| Fly out | **Good** — 1 good unit |
| Ground out | **Good** — 1 good unit |
| Walk | **Bad** — 1 bad unit |
| Hit | **Bad** — 1 bad unit (+ more if runs score) |

**Delta** = good units minus bad units for the inning or outing.  
Positive delta = you won more fights than you lost.

---

## Why Every At-Bat Has Value

Season stats average you into one number. EABR remembers **each fight**.

That matters because:

- You can pitch well and give up runs (bad luck)
- You can pitch poorly and escape (good luck)
- Your coach can show you **both** truths

You are not "a 4.50 ERA." You are "a pitcher who won 14 of 22 at-bats last outing with one bad cluster."

---

## Why Not Every Out Is Equal (But Every Out Counts)

All defensive outs earn **one good unit** at the base level:

| Out type | Good units | Outs recorded |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| Strikeout | 1 | 1 |
| Fly out | 1 | 1 |
| Ground out | 1 | 1 |

**Why the same?** EABR measures **who won the at-bat**, not how pretty the out looked.

A strikeout and a weak groundout are both wins. You do not get extra good units for a strikeout — but you should still want strikeouts when they fit the plan.

---

## Why Double Plays Matter

A ground-ball double play:

- **1 good unit** (you won the at-bat)
- **2 outs** (you helped end the inning faster)

You do not get two good units for one plate appearance — that would double-count one trip to the mound. The **extra out** is the reward: you escaped a jam and shortened the inning.

As a pitcher, think: *"I pitched to contact, got the ground ball, and my defense turned two."* That is a huge win even though EABR shows one good unit.

---

## Why RBI Allowed Matters

When a hit or walk **scores runs**, each run adds an extra **bad unit** on top of the base bad unit.

| Play | Bad units |
|------|-----------|
| Walk, bases empty | 1 |
| Walk, bases loaded (1 run scores) | 1 + 1 = **2** |
| Solo home run | 1 + 1 = **2** |
| Grand slam | 1 + 4 = **5** |

**Why?** A single with no one on hurts less than a single that plates two. EABR reflects **damage**, not just "they got a hit."

This is not saying you are a bad pitcher. It is saying **that at-bat was costly** — and costly at-bats deserve honest weight.

---

## Why Stranding Runners Matters

When you record the **third out** and runners are still on base, you get **+1 good unit for each stranded runner**.

| Situation | Example good units on the final out |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| Strikeout, bases empty | 1 |
| Strikeout, runners on 2nd and 3rd | 1 + 2 = **3** |

**Why?** You competed with traffic and **did not let them score**. Coaches have always valued that. EABR puts a number on it.

Stranding runners is not luck — it is execution under pressure. PSIS rewards it.

---

## Hits Are Not All Painted the Same in Your Head — But the Base Rule Is Simple

A weak single and a line-drive single both start as **1 bad unit**. Damage from runners scoring shows up in **RBI bad units**.

Your coach may talk about contact quality in film session. EABR starts with: **did the hitter reach or not?**

---

## Fly Outs and Hard Contact

A rocket to the warning track that is caught is still **1 good unit**. You won the at-bat.

If that frustrates you — good. It means you care about contact quality. Talk to your coach about it. The log still credits the out.

---

## What EABR Does Not Say

| EABR does not… | Because… |
|----------------|----------|
| Measure your velocity | That is training technology |
| Know if your mechanics were right | That is video |
| Know if the hitter was elite | Everyone counts the same |
| Judge your heart | You are not a number |

EABR says: **who won this at-bat, and how costly was it?**

---

## Your Cheat Sheet

```
WIN the at-bat     → good unit
LOSE the at-bat    → bad unit
RUNS score on you  → extra bad units (RBI)
STRAND runners     → extra good units (LOB on 3rd out)
DELTA              → good minus bad (your net)
```
