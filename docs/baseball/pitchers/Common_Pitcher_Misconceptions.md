# Common Pitcher Misconceptions

Straight answers when the numbers confuse you.

---

## "I struck out eight hitters. Why wasn't my EABR great?"

**Strikeouts are good units** — one each. But EABR counts **every at-bat**.

| Possible explanation |
|---------------------|
| Walks and hits added bad units |
| Runs scored added **RBI bad units** on top of hits |
| You won strikeouts but lost the battles in between |
| One bad inning stacked damage |

**Example:** 8 Ks = 8 good units. 4 walks + 2 singles that scored 3 runs could add 4 + 2 + 3 = 9 bad units (simplified). Delta goes negative despite the strikeouts.

**Takeaway:** Strikeouts help. They are not the only at-bat.

---

## "My ERA went down but my EABR didn't improve."

**ERA and EABR measure different things.**

| ERA | EABR |
|-----|------|
| Runs allowed | At-bats won vs lost |
| Defense and luck included | Each confrontation logged |
| One big inning hurts a lot | Inning deltas show where |

You might have:

- Stranded runners (good for EABR) but given up fewer cheap runs (ERA down)
- Gotten lucky on balls in play (ERA down) while losing more at-bats (EABR flat)
- Pitched in less damaging situations

**Takeaway:** ERA down is good for the team. EABR asks: **are you winning more fights?** Both can be true at once.

---

## "I only gave up runs in one bad inning."

**That is exactly why at-bat and inning logging exists.**

| Whole-game ERA | Inning deltas |
|----------------|---------------|
| One inning dominates memory | Other innings may show positive delta |
| Feels like a bad start | Data shows **where** it broke |

**Takeaway:** You are not "a bad pitcher" — you had **a bad inning**. Fix that cluster with your coach. Do not throw away the good innings.

---

## "Why does PSIS care about runners left on base?"

Because **stranding runners is pitching**.

When you get the third out with runners on second and third:

- You prevented runs from scoring
- Coaches have always valued that
- EABR adds **good units** for each stranded runner

**Takeaway:** LOB credit rewards **competing in jams**, not just clean innings.

---

## "Does PSIS think strikeouts are everything?"

**No.**

| Also worth good units |
|----------------------|
| Fly outs |
| Ground outs |
| Double plays (1 good unit + 2 outs) |
| Stranded runners |

Strikeouts are the cleanest win. **Contact outs win at-bats too.** Pitchers who pitch to contact can have excellent EABR.

---

## "A home run is one pitch — why so many bad units?"

A solo homer = **1 base bad unit + 1 RBI bad unit = 2 bad units**.

A grand slam = **1 + 4 = 5 bad units**.

**Why?** Damage matters. One swing can change the game — EABR reflects that cost honestly.

**Takeaway:** Homers hurt the log. They do not end your development. **Next at-bat.**

---

## "I got a ground-ball double play. Why only one good unit?"

You only get **one plate appearance** — one at-bat win. The **second out** shows up in the inning (you escaped faster), not as a second good unit.

**Takeaway:** DP is still a huge win. The score reflects at-bat wins; the inning reflects outs.

---

## "PSIS hates me when I give up a hard-hit fly out that's caught."

A caught fly ball = **1 good unit** — you won the at-bat.

If the ball was smoked, talk to your coach about pitch location or sequencing. The log still credits the out.

---

## "My coach says I pitched well but my delta was negative."

Possible truths **at the same time**:

- Your **process** was good (execution, compete)
- **Outcomes** went against you (hits on good pitches)
- Or walks/hits in key spots stacked bad units

Film + EABR together tell the story. Neither alone is complete.

---

## "Is negative delta a failing grade?"

**No.** It is a snapshot of **who won more at-bats that day**.

| Response |
|----------|
| One negative outing | Learn and adjust |
| Negative trend over a month | Real work needed — with coach support |
| Positive trend | Keep going |

You are not graded as a person. You are **informed** as a competitor.

---

## "Do I need PSIS to get better?"

**No.** You need:

- Honest review
- A plan
- Repetition
- A coach who tells you the truth

PSIS (or EABR on paper) just makes the truth **easier to see**.
