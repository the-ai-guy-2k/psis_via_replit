# Pitch Sequencing Philosophy

Why PSIS treats pitch sequencing as a baseball skill — not just pitch execution.

---

## Execution vs Sequencing

| Concept | Question |
|---------|----------|
| **Execution** | Did the pitch do what you intended? (location, movement, velocity) |
| **Sequencing** | Did you set up this pitch with what came before? |

A perfectly executed slider can be hit hard if the hitter was sitting on it. A mediocre slider can miss a bat if the fastball inside established the outer half.

**PSIS hypothesis:** Both matter — but sequencing is what separates pitchers who survive from pitchers who dominate.

---

## Why Sequences Are Logged

PSIS records the ordered pitches thrown before each plate appearance ends (e.g., `FB-FB-SL`).

That string is not decoration. It is evidence of:

- **Planning** — Did the pitcher have a sequence in mind?
- **Adjustment** — Did he change speed, eye level, or location?
- **Repeatability** — Which sequences produce good EABR outcomes over time?

When the same sequence appears across multiple good at-bats, coaches have data — not just memory.

---

## Setting Hitters Up

Good sequencing follows a logic chain:

```
Establish a pitch → Change speed or location → Finish with intent
```

Examples coaches recognize:

| Setup | Finish | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| Fastball inside | Slider away | Expand plate; induce chase |
| Fastball up | Changeup down | Change eye level |
| Slider away | Fastball in | Freeze or jam |
| Off-speed early | Fastball two-strike | Put-away |

PSIS does not judge whether the setup was correct. It **preserves the sequence** so coaches can review whether the setup worked.

---

## Creating Weak Contact

Not every success is a strikeout. Sequencing often aims for:

- Ground balls on the pull side
- Lazy fly balls
- Foul balls that burn pitches but delay damage

EABR credits the **outcome** (good unit). The **sequence** explains how the pitcher got there.

A session where `FB-CH-GB` sequences correlate with positive delta tells a development story strikeout rate alone cannot.

---

## Using Previous Pitches

Hitters adjust within an at-bat. So do good pitchers.

| Count / situation | Sequencing idea |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Ahead 0–2 | Waste pitch or put-away? |
| Even 2–2 | Best pitch or setup pitch? |
| Behind 2–0 | Challenge or nibble? |

PSIS logs the sequence as thrown — coaches interpret whether the **previous pitch** did its job.

---

## Changing Eye Level

Vertical sequencing disrupts timing:

- Up fastball → down breaking ball
- Down sinker → elevated four-seam

Eye-level changes are among the most teachable sequencing skills. Logging sequences lets coaches ask: *When we changed eye level, what happened to EABR?*

---

## Changing Speed

Speed differential is sequencing in time:

- Hard-soft-hard patterns
- Back-to-back changeups (rare but memorable)
- Fastball after two off-speed pitches

Velocity itself is not in PSIS. **Order and pitch type labels** are — enough to discuss speed change as a plan, not a radar gun reading.

---

## Executing a Plan

Every pitcher should have a **plan per hitter**:

1. Primary attack
2. Secondary pitch
3. Put-away option

Sequencing is the log of that plan in action. When the plan fails, EABR records a bad unit — and the sequence shows what was tried.

When the plan succeeds, coaches can reinforce: *"This is the sequence that works against this type of hitter."*

---

## What PSIS Does Not Claim About Sequences

| Claim | PSIS stance |
|-------|-------------|
| One "best" sequence exists | False — context matters |
| Sequences alone predict success | No — outcomes still depend on execution |
| AI will choose sequences | Not in scope — coach decides |
| Every pitch type is captured in detail | Coach uses shorthand (FB, SL, CH, etc.) |

---

## Sequences and Development

Over a season, sequence aggregation reveals:

- **Best sequences** — highest average EABR delta
- **Worst sequences** — patterns to avoid or fix

These rankings are coaching conversation starters, not commandments.

---

## The Philosophy in One Sentence

> **Pitching is not throwing pitches — it is throwing the right pitch after the right pitch. PSIS remembers the chain so coaches can teach the chain.**
