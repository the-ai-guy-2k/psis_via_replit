# Limitations of the Model

What PSIS cannot know — and why coaching will always remain necessary.

---

## This Document Exists on Purpose

A philosophy that cannot admit weakness is not trustworthy.

PSIS and EABR are **coaching tools**, not complete descriptions of pitching. This section lists what the model deliberately ignores or simplifies.

---

## Mechanics

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Arm slot | Not logged |
| Hip-shoulder separation | Not logged |
| Delivery timing | Not logged |
| Fatigue mechanics | Not detected |

**Why:** Mechanics are seen on video, not in outcome buttons. EABR records results; coaches fix delivery.

---

## Velocity

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| MPH per pitch | Not in PSIS |
| Velocity drop within outing | Not tracked |
| Velo vs command tradeoff | Coach judgment |

**Why:** Radar data belongs in tech-enabled programs. PSIS sequence shorthand (FB, SL) can imply pitch type without speed.

---

## Spin and Movement

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Spin rate | Not logged |
| Horizontal/vertical break | Not logged |
| Pitch design metrics | Not logged |

**Why:** Statcast and Rapsodo answer movement questions. EABR answers *did the at-bat work* questions.

---

## Weather and Environment

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Wind | Not modeled |
| Humidity / ball carry | Not modeled |
| Dome vs outdoor | Not modeled |
| Mound conditions | Not modeled |

**Why:** Context would multiply fields beyond practical logging. Coaches adjust mentally.

---

## Defense

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Defensive quality | Not rated |
| Shift positioning | Not logged |
| Catcher framing | Not modeled |
| Errors | Not a separate category |

**Philosophy:** EABR classifies the **outcome reached**. A chopper that eats up the shortstop may still be logged as a hit — coach discusses defense separately.

**MVP simplification:** On defensive outs, runners do not advance (no sac fly, no fielder's choice modeling). Real baseball is messier.

---

## Luck and BABIP

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Exit velocity | Not logged |
| Launch angle | Not logged |
| "Should have been an out" | Not automated |

A lineout and a bloop single may both be recorded as offensive success (hit) or defensive success (fly out) based on **what happened**, not expected stats.

Coaches bring xwOBA and video. EABR brings consistency.

---

## Mental State

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Confidence | Not measured |
| Focus between innings | Not measured |
| Game pressure | Not indexed |

**Why:** Psychology is real and coaching-critical — but not reducible to good/bad units without harming trust.

---

## Hitter Quality

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Opponent strength | Not weighted |
| Scouting report execution | Coach judgment |
| Lineup position | Not logged |

Facing the 3-hole hitter and facing the 9-hole hitter count the same in EABR. Coaches contextualize externally.

---

## Count and Leverage

| Gap | Reality |
|-----|---------|
| Ball-strike count per pitch | Not fully modeled |
| Leverage index | Not calculated |
| Game score situation | Beyond base state |

Walk with bases loaded is context-aware. Walk on 3-0 in a blowout is treated the same at the unit level.

---

## Base-Running and Situational Baseball

| Not modeled | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Sacrifice fly | Runner tags, scores — MVP treats many outs as non-advancing |
| Fielder's choice | Not distinguished |
| Stolen bases | Not tracked |
| Balks | Not tracked |
| Hit-by-pitch | Not in current outcome set |

These are **known MVP boundaries**, not philosophical rejections. Future versions may expand.

---

## Pitch-Level Quality Within an At-Bat

EABR evaluates the **end** of the at-bat, not each pitch.

A 12-pitch battle ending in a walk is one bad unit — same as a four-pitch walk. Coaches who value efficiency must note it separately.

---

## Gaming the Metric

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Pitchers avoid contact to chase Ks | Coach sets team philosophy |
| Staff mis-classify outcomes | Training and review |
| Ignoring bad contact that falls | Honest logging culture |

Any metric can be gamed. Culture prevents it — not software.

---

## Why Coaching Will Always Remain Necessary

| PSIS provides | Coach provides |
|---------------|----------------|
| Consistent at-bat log | Judgment |
| Aggregated trends | Motivation |
| Sequence memory | Mechanical fixes |
| Damage and LOB weighting | Game planning |
| Shared language | Humanity |

> **PSIS is a notebook that never forgets and always adds the same way. The coach is still the author of the development plan.**

---

## Invitation to Disagree

If these limitations make EABR unusable for your program, that is valid feedback.

If they define where PSIS **fits** rather than where it **fails**, the tool earns its place in the bullpen.
