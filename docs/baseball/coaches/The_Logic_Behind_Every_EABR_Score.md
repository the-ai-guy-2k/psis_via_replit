# The Logic Behind Every EABR Score

The heart of PSIS baseball philosophy — every scoring decision, explained for coaches.

---

## The Unit System (Read This First)

Every plate appearance produces:

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Good units** | Pitcher-favorable credit |
| **Bad units** | Pitcher-unfavorable debit |
| **Delta** | Good − Bad for this at-bat (contributes to inning/session totals) |

### Universal rules

1. **Defense outcome** → 1 good unit (base), 0 bad units (before LOB)
2. **Offense outcome** → 1 bad unit (base) + 1 additional bad unit per RBI
3. **Inning ends** → +1 good unit per runner left on base, credited on the **third-out at-bat**
4. **Delta** = good units − bad units (runs scored are reflected through RBI bad units, not subtracted again)

---

## Defense Outcomes (Good for the Pitcher)

### Strikeout

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Units** | 1 good, 0 bad |
| **Outs** | 1 |

**Baseball reasoning:** The pitcher missed the bat or induced a swing through. No ball in play. Maximum pitcher control.

**Rewards:** Execution, put-away pitches, two-strike approach.  
**Discourages:** N/A — this is the cleanest win.

---

### Fly Out

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Units** | 1 good, 0 bad (infield or outfield location) |
| **Outs** | 1 |

**Baseball reasoning:** The batter put the ball in the air and the defense recorded an out. Pop-ups and routine flies are pitcher wins even when contact was hard.

**Follow-up recorded:** Catch location (infield vs outfield) — for coaching notes only; **does not change units**.

**Rewards:** Elevated fastballs, swing-miss counts that produce lazy fly balls, pitch design that induces top-side contact.  
**Discourages:** Mistaking "deep fly out" for failure — EABR still credits the out.

**Honest caveat:** A warning-track fly is still an out in EABR. Coaches may mentally weight it differently in conversation. That nuance lives in notes and video, not the unit score.

---

### Ground Out — Single Play

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Units** | 1 good, 0 bad |
| **Outs** | 1 |

**Baseball reasoning:** Batter hit a ground ball; one out recorded. Contact management success.

**Rewards:** Sinkers, two-seamers, sliders down, pitch-to-contact approach.  
**Discourages:** Chasing strikeouts when a ground ball achieves the goal.

---

### Ground Out — Double Play

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Units** | 1 good, 0 bad |
| **Outs** | 2 |

**Baseball reasoning:** The pitcher induced a ground ball in a situation where two outs were possible. A major inning escape.

**Why not 2 good units?** EABR separates **at-bat wins** (one per plate appearance) from **outs recorded** (tracked for inning state). Double plays are spectacular — the extra out advances the inning, which is its own reward. Giving two good units would double-count one plate appearance.

**Rewards:** Pitching to contact with runners on, sinker/slider execution, trusting defense.  
**Discourages:** Avoiding contact at all costs when a grounder is the right play.

---

### Ground Out — Triple Play

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Units** | 1 good, 0 bad |
| **Outs** | Up to remaining outs in inning (capped at 3 total) |

**Baseball reasoning:** Rare, inning-ending brilliance. Same unit rule as double play — one at-bat, one base good unit.

## Offense Outcomes (Bad for the Pitcher)

### Walk

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Base units** | 0 good, 1 bad |
| **RBI bad units** | +1 per run forced home (bases loaded walk = 1 RBI) |
| **Outs** | 0 |

**Baseball reasoning:** The pitcher lost the at-bat without a hit — free baserunner. Damage escalates when walks force runs.

**Example — bases empty walk:** 1 bad unit.  
**Example — bases loaded walk:** 1 base bad + 1 RBI bad = **2 bad units**.

**Rewards (inverse):** Strike-throwing, ahead-in-count aggression.  
**Discourages:** Nibbling, fear of contact, falling behind hitters.

---

### Hit — Single

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Base units** | 0 good, 1 bad |
| **RBI bad units** | +1 per run that scores on the play |
| **Outs** | 0 |

**Baseball reasoning:** The batter reached safely. Damage depends on runners scoring.

**Example — solo single, bases empty:** 1 bad unit.  
**Example — single scores one runner from second:** 1 base + 1 RBI = **2 bad units**.

**Rewards (inverse):** Weak contact allowed when fielders convert (would be ground out — good unit).  
**Discourages:** Middle-middle mistakes, hanging breaking balls.

**Note:** Hard single and weak single both record 1 base bad unit. Contact quality is a coaching conversation, not a unit multiplier.

---

### Hit — Double

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Base units** | 0 good, 1 bad |
| **RBI bad units** | +1 per run scoring |
| **Outs** | 0 |

**Baseball reasoning:** Extra-base hit — more advancement, often more RBI damage. Base unit stays 1; **RBI units carry the extra cost**.

**Example — double scores two:** 1 base + 2 RBI = **3 bad units**.

---

### Hit — Triple

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Base units** | 0 good, 1 bad |
| **RBI bad units** | +1 per run scoring |
| **Outs** | 0 |

Same logic as double — damage through runs scored, not hit-type multiplier.

---

### Hit — Home Run

| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Base units** | 0 good, 1 bad |
| **RBI bad units** | +1 per run scoring (including the batter) |
| **Outs** | 0 |

**Example — solo home run:** 1 base + 1 RBI = **2 bad units**.  
**Example — grand slam:** 1 base + 4 RBI = **5 bad units**.

**Baseball reasoning:** Maximum damage. EABR punishes runs directly through RBI units rather than treating all hits equally.

**Rewards (inverse):** Keeping the ball in the park — fly outs and ground outs.  
**Discourages:** Mistakes over the plate in hitter counts.

---

## RBI — Why It Works This Way

**RBI in EABR** = runs that scored because of this at-bat (from base-state logic).

| Philosophy | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **Damage matters** | A single that plates two hurts more than a single with bases empty |
| **No double penalty** | Runs are not also subtracted separately from delta — RBI bad units already capture damage |
| **Walks can hurt** | Forced-in runs on walks receive RBI weighting |

**What EABR does not do:** Credit or debit for RBIs on subsequent batters' hits (that is their at-bat).

## LOB (Runners Left on Base)

When the **third out** ends the inning, count runners still on base.

| Runners left | Good units added to completing at-bat |
|--------------|---------------------------------------|
| 0 | 0 |
| 1 | +1 |
| 2 | +2 |
| 3 | +3 |

**Baseball reasoning:** Stranding runners is a pitching success. The inning ended without those runners scoring.

**Credited on:** The at-bat that records the third out (often a ground out or strikeout — already 1 good unit, plus LOB).

**Example:** Strikeout ends inning with runners on 2nd and 3rd → 1 good (strikeout) + 2 good (LOB) = **3 good units** on one at-bat.

**Rewards:** Pitching out of jams, trusting stuff with runners on, avoiding the big inning.  
**Discourages:** Giving up when traffic appears — LOB is explicitly rewarded.

---

## Runs Scored

Runs are **tracked for display** but enter the unit system through **RBI bad units**, not a separate subtraction.

This replaced an older formula that subtracted runs from delta. The current philosophy: **if runs scored on the hitter's at-bat, RBI units express the damage.**

---

## Errors and Fielding Influence

**EABR does not model errors separately.**

| Situation | EABR approach |
|-----------|---------------|
| Error on would-be out | Coach classifies the **actual outcome** reached |
| Defensive gem saves a hit | Still an offensive outcome if batter reached |

**Philosophy:** EABR is a pitching-coach framework, not official scoring. Coaches log what happened from their vantage — and discuss defense separately.

**Limitation acknowledged:** This is imperfect. See [Limitations_Of_The_Model.md](./Limitations_Of_The_Model.md).

## What Does NOT Change the Score

| Recorded detail | Affects units? |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Fly-out location (infield/outfield) | No |
| Hit type (single vs HR) base unit | No — still 1 base bad unit |
| Double play vs single ground out base unit | No — still 1 good unit |
| Pitch sequence text | No — used for pattern analysis separately |
| Coach notes | No |

## Inning and Session Rollups

| Level | Calculation |
|-------|-------------|
| **Inning delta** | Sum of at-bat deltas in that inning |
| **Inning fraction** | Sum good ÷ sum bad |
| **Session delta** | Sum across all innings in the session |
| **Session fraction** | Total good ÷ total bad |
