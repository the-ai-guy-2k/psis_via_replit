## Findings

### Strengths

1. **Coffee-and-ballpark tone** — `Why_I_Built_PSIS.md` and `Old_Head_Baseball_Essay.md` read as personal narrative, not product marketing.

2. **ERA and wins defended** — `When_Traditional_Stats_Get_It_Right.md` gives traditional metrics their due over full seasons; no dismissive analytics rhetoric.

3. **Box score respect** — Documents call the box score a "masterpiece of compression" and a "good servant" — criticism is of **omission**, not of tradition itself.

4. **Pitching to contact honored** — Ground outs and fly outs treated as pitcher wins; strikeouts not elevated as sole virtue.

5. **No conversion pressure** — README promise, Common Questions, and essay closing explicitly welcome disagreement.

6. **Minimal jargon** — EABR mechanics largely avoided; "who won the at-bat" plain language used throughout.

7. **Historical touchstones** — References to scorekeepers, broadcast rhythm, complete games, Mathewson/Maddux era without pedantry.

8. **Essay stands alone** — `Old_Head_Baseball_Essay.md` contains no software instructions or technical vocabulary.
