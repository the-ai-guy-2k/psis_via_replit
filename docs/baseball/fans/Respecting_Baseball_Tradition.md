# Respecting Baseball Tradition

PSIS was not built to fix baseball. It was built to **listen** to how baseball has always been understood by the people who care most.

---

## What We Respect Without Apology

### ERA

Earned run average is the language of pitching. It has survived a century because **runs matter**. PSIS does not ask you to ignore ERA. It asks you to remember what happened **before** those runs got painted on the line.

### Wins

A pitcher who gives his team a chance to win is doing his job. Wins are a team stat — everyone knows that — but **competing deep into the game** still means something in every dugout in America. PSIS does not mock the W column.

### Complete games

Finishing what you started is an act of trust between pitcher, catcher, manager, and defense. The complete game is not obsolete as an **ideal** — even in an era of bullpens. PSIS cares about every inning of that effort, not just the final number.

### Command

Throwing strikes. Working ahead in the count. Making the hitter swing at your pitch. That is not analytics. That is **pitching**.

### Changing speeds

The fastball sets up the change-up. The change-up makes the fastball jump. Fans have known this since the dead ball era. PSIS logs sequences because **changing speeds is baseball thinking**, not spreadsheet thinking.

### Pitch sequencing

*Fastball in, slider away.* You have heard it in every broadcast booth. Sequencing is the pitcher's chess game. PSIS treats it as central — because it always was.

### Fielding

Pitchers pitch to contact. Defense turns double plays. A ground-ball pitcher lives on his infield. PSIS does not pretend defense does not exist — it records **outs and traffic** the way a fan in the stands does, then lets the conversation include fielders honestly.

### Baseball instincts

The veteran who smells a rally. The catcher who knows the hitter will chase. The manager who visits the mound because the rhythm is off. No system replaces instinct. PSIS is a **memory aid** for what instinct already noticed.

---

## Built On Baseball — Not Against It

| Old-school truth | How PSIS relates |
|------------------|------------------|
| "Pitching is one hitter at a time" | PSIS starts at the at-bat |
| "He pitched to contact" | Ground outs and fly outs count as pitcher wins |
| "He stranded runners" | Stranding runners is rewarded in the framework |
| "That homer hurt" | Damage is weighted honestly |
| "Know what you threw before" | Sequences are logged |

Nothing here requires you to stop trusting your eyes. It requires you to believe that **what you see can be written down** the same way twice — so the kid on the mound and the coach in the dugout are talking about the same game.

---

## What PSIS Does Not Ask You to Give Up

- Your skepticism of fads
- Your love of the box score
- Your stories about players from decades ago
- Your right to disagree with a number

We ask only this: **if the game is really a series of at-bats, maybe the record should say so.**

That is tradition extended — not tradition replaced.
