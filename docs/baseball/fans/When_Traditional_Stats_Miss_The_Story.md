# When Traditional Stats Miss the Story

Two pitchers. Same ERA. Different nights. Different truth.

---

## Example One: Identical Lines, Opposite Feel

**Pitcher A — Tuesday**

| Inning | What happened |
|--------|---------------|
| 1–4 | Quick innings, weak contact, two strikeouts |
| 5 | Walk, single, bloop, two runs — inning unravels |
| 6 | Ground out, fly out, strikeout — back in control |

**Line:** 6 IP, 3 ER, ERA for the night looks "okay."

**What you saw:** Won most at-bats. One bad cluster. **Felt like a good outing with one scar.**

---

**Pitcher B — Wednesday**

| Inning | What happened |
|--------|---------------|
| 1–6 | Constant traffic — hits, walks, hard fly outs caught at the wall |
| Every inning | Runners in scoring position |

**Line:** 6 IP, 3 ER — **same earned runs as Pitcher A.**

**What you saw:** Lucky to escape. **Felt like trouble all night.**

The ERA for that game treats them the same. Your eyes do not.

---

## Example Two: Escaped Jams vs Created Them

**Pitcher C**

- Bases loaded twice
- Strikeout and double play both times
- Stranded six runners

**Pitcher D**

- Clean innings early
- Fifth inning: walk, walk, single, grand slam

Same runs allowed might not even happen — but imagine both allow **three earned runs** on the season line over two starts.

Pitcher C **competed**. Pitcher D **collapsed in one frame**.

Traditional stats over time may sort them out. **That night's conversation** should not wait until October.

---

## Example Three: Pitching to Contact vs Getting Lucky

**Ground-ball pitcher:** Twelve ground outs, three hits — all singles, all well pitched.

**Fly-ball pitcher:** Twelve fly outs, two homers — both on mistakes middle of the zone.

Similar WHIP. Different **quality of contact** and different **mistakes**.

Fans know which one they trust next week. A single WHIP column does not always say.

---

## Why Context Matters

Context is not a fancy word. It is **what you remember**:

- Who was on base
- How the out was made
- What pitch preceded the damage
- Whether the inning **could have been worse**

PSIS records context at the level a diligent scorekeeper always could — **who won each at-bat**, whether runners scored, whether runners were stranded when the inning ended.

Not because tradition was stupid — because **tradition was watching the whole game**, and the summary line was never enough for the people in the know.

---

## The Respectful Point

We are not saying ERA is useless. We are saying:

> **Two pitchers with the same ERA on the same night can pitch very different games.**

Any fan who has sat through both outings already believes that. PSIS is built for that belief.
