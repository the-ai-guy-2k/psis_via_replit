# Pitching Is More Than Statistics

Long before anyone said "spin rate," pitchers were artists.

---

## What the Great Ones Always Did

### Change eye level

Up fastball, down breaking ball. The hitter's swing changes when his eyes move. Broadcasters said it in the 1960s. Catchers said it in the 1920s.

### Set the hitter up

You do not start with your out pitch. You **earn** it. Two fastballs inside, then the slider away. The crowd does not always see the setup. The hitter feels it.

### Sequence pitches

Pitching is memory — yours and theirs. *What did I throw last time? What will he expect now?* That is baseball IQ, not a spreadsheet.

### Know when to challenge

Sometimes you beat a hitter with a letter-high fastball because **he does not expect you to have the guts**. Old school. Still true.

### Know when not to

Sometimes you pitch around the four-hole hitter in a tight game. Sometimes you nibble. That is strategy — not cowardice.

### Read swings

Did he pull off? Is he late? Did he foul off your best pitch? Your eyes tell you before any machine does.

### Compete

Runners on third. Crowd loud. Count full. The pitcher who wants the ball — that is half of pitching, and **no stat has ever captured it fully**.

PSIS did not invent any of this. PSIS **remembers** it alongside the result — so the craft and the outcome stay together.

---

## Statistics Describe. They Do Not Pitch.

ERA tells you runs. Strikeouts tell you misses. WHIP tells you traffic.

None of them throw a pitch. None of them hold a runner on third. None of them know the batter just fouled off two sliders and is guessing fastball.

**Pitching is more than statistics** — and the best fans have always known that. PSIS agrees.

---

## Where PSIS Adds Something Modest

It asks: after all that craft — the sequencing, the compete, the reads — **who won the at-bat?**

That question is as old as the mound. We just think it deserves a consistent answer when the outing is over.
