# When Traditional Stats Get It Right

Fair is fair. The numbers on the back of the card survived for a reason.

---

## ERA — When It Works Well

**Earned run average** answers a real question: *How many runs is this pitcher allowing, on average, over a full season?*

It works well when:

| Condition | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Large sample size | Luck and one bad night average out |
| Similar ballparks and eras | Comparison is meaningful |
| You want the bottom line | Front offices and fans care about runs |

When a veteran posts a 3.20 ERA over 180 innings, **he probably helped his team**. PSIS does not argue with that.

ERA is the **season story**. PSIS is the **night's story**. Both can be true.

---

## Wins — When They Matter

Wins are a team stat. Everyone over twelve years old knows that.

They still matter because:

- Pitchers who go deep give bullpens rest
- Pitchers who keep teams close **change the game**
- In a tight pennant race, **getting the W** is remembered

PSIS does not replace wins. It helps explain **how** a pitcher earned or did not earn the chance to win on a given night.

---

## WHIP — When Traffic Tells the Truth

**Walks plus hits per inning** is simple and honest about pressure.

High WHIP usually means:

- Too many baserunners
- Too many stressful innings
- Too much dependence on defense and luck

When WHIP and ERA both look bad over a season, **the pitcher probably struggled**. Old-school instinct and the numbers agree.

---

## Strikeouts — When Missing Bats Matters

Strikeouts never go out of style:

- No ball in play
- No rally starter
- Maximum pitcher control

A strikeout pitcher with swing-and-miss stuff is **winning at-bats cleanly**. PSIS credits that the same way any fan would — as a pitcher win.

---

## Complete Games and Innings Eaten

Going seven, eight, or nine **still signals trust**:

- Stamina
- Efficiency
- Willingness to compete through traffic

The bullpen era changed roles — not the **value of a pitcher who finishes his work**.

---

## The Historical Record

Ty Cobb's average. Walter Johnson's ERA. Nolan Ryan's strikeouts.

**Tradition is data.** We are not throwing out a century of context. We are saying that **tonight's start** might need a finer grain than one line — the same way a box score never replaced watching the game.

---

## When to Trust the Traditional Line

| Trust it more when… | Example |
|---------------------|---------|
| Season-long sample | 150+ innings |
| Numbers match your eyes | Bad ERA, lots of hard contact |
| Role is stable | Starter vs reliever compared fairly |

PSIS adds detail. It does not ask you to distrust a summer's worth of evidence.
