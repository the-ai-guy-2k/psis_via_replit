# Common Old-School Questions

Straight answers. No talking down.

---

## "Are you saying ERA doesn't matter?"

**No.**

ERA matters — especially over a season. It tells you how many runs a pitcher allowed. Runs win and lose games.

PSIS asks a **different question for a different moment**: *On this night, who won more at-bats — the pitcher or the hitters?*

ERA is the season. PSIS is the story inside the start. **Both.**

---

## "Is this just another made-up stat?"

It is **old thinking with a consistent scorecard**.

Fans and coaches have always said:

- "He won that battle"
- "He pitched better than his line"
- "He stranded a ton of guys"

PSIS gives those sentences a shared format. If that feels new, the **ideas** are not.

---

## "What about complete games?"

Complete games still mean **stamina, trust, and finishing**. PSIS does not grade pitchers on how many innings they throw.

It cares about **what happened in each inning** — whether the pitcher won more fights than he lost while he was out there.

A seven-inning start with positive at-bat wins is a good night. A nine-inning start where he was behind hitters all night might not be — even if he survived.

**Innings matter. Quality inside those innings matters too.**

---

## "What about pitching to contact?"

**Pitching to contact is real baseball.**

Ground outs and fly outs are **pitcher wins** in PSIS — same as strikeouts at the base level. A sinkerballer who induces weak rollers is doing his job.

PSIS is not a strikeout cult. It is an **at-bat cult** — whoever wins the confrontation.

---

## "What about defense?"

Defense matters. Always has.

PSIS does not rate fielders. It records **outs and baserunners** the way a fan does. When a grounder is booted, the story gets messy — same as in real life. Coaches and fans still talk about whether the **pitch was right**.

No system fixes blame between pitcher and shortstop perfectly. **Neither does the box score.**

---

## "Can a computer really understand baseball?"

**No.**

A computer does not smell a rally or hear a bat. People do.

PSIS is a **notebook** — not an oracle. Coaches and fans still judge contact quality, heart, and context. The computer just adds without forgetting.

If you trust a scorekeeper in the third row, you can trust a shared log the same way — **as memory, not as wisdom.**

---

## "Is this analytics?"

Not the kind that puts dots on a stadium chart.

No launch angle lectures. No expected stats required.

PSIS is closer to **keeping score carefully** than to inventing a new science. If you hate "analytics," you might still hate the acronym — but the **baseball** underneath is what your grandfather argued about in the parking lot.

---

## "Why should I care if I'm just a fan?"

You do not have to care.

Care if:

- Your kid pitches and you want to understand what the coach sees
- You argue about starters after games and want **shared facts**
- You love the **inside game** — sequences, jams, escapes

Otherwise, enjoy the sunshine. Baseball does not require homework.

---

## "I still don't agree with everything."

**Good.**

We are not asking for conversion. We are asking for a fair hearing:

> Traditional stats summarize. At-bats tell the story. PSIS remembers the story.

If that makes baseball sense — even with reservations — we are on the same side of loving the game.
